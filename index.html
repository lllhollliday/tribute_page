ALTER EGOS THROUGH THE YEARS

  • Ziggy Stardust, the most well known character, was created by David Bowie for the 1972 concept album: The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars. The character is an alien sent to earth, to bring a positive message of hope in the last five years of its existence. David Bowie played as the character on the tour of the same name, and retired it on stage in July 1973.
  • Aladdin Sane is a David Bowie persona associated with the album of the same name, released in 1973.
  • Bowie portrayed the thin white duke persona from 1975 to 1976. The character appears in the 1976 album Station to Station, in the film: The Man Who Fell To Earth, on the 1976 Isolar tour, on the cover of the album Low and in the song Lazarus as a final goodbye to the character. Bowie played this character at the peak of his drug use while living in Los Angeles. The character was known for pro-fascist statements made in interviews.
  • Halloween Jack was created for the 1974 Diamond Dogs album and tour. He appears on the front cover of the album, and is mentioned in the songs. He is described as being a "real cool cat" who lives in Hunger City. Some of this is inspired by the novel: 1984 by George Orwell. He has a very similar appearance to the other personas Ziggy Stardust and Aladdin Sane - all three had shaved eyebrows and a red mullet haircut. David appeared as this persona in the Diamond Dogs recording sessions and early 1974 interviews, before changing into the more Soul Man-ish appearance for the tour and rest of the year.
  • The Soul Man was created for the Diamond Dogs tour. Specifically for the soul part of the tour, that included songs from the then forthcoming, R&B and soul album: Young Americans. However, his first appearance was as a DJ in the song Starman From The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars. It wasn't until David got cast to be in the movie: The Man Who Fell To Earth, that The Soul Man was retired.
